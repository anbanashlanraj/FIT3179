Navigating the Corporate Cosmos:
A Visual Exploration of Forbes Global 2000

The Forbes Global 2000 ranks the largest companies in the world using four key indicators namely sales, profits, assets and market value. The data visualised shows the performance of the countries in 2022 with the United States leading the charts with a whopping 584 companies of the 2000, and China coming in second with 297 companies. There are 57 countries represented by the publicly traded companies on the Forbes Global 2000 list.

Global Forbes 2000 Map

By using the interactive slider, you can explore the vast divide when it comes to market dominance where there are just a handful of companies which dominate the market globally. Note that beyond the 100 companies mark, only Japan, China and the U.S. make it through; attesting to how much geographical influence there is on the global market.

Forbes 2000 Companies by Industry

The chart above shows us the breakdown by industry to which the Global 2000 companies belong to. It is clear that the banking sector tops the charts for being the industry with the most number of companies. Among the near-300 companies, 94 of which are from East Asia suggesting a strong dominance of this list from financial hubs such as China and Hong Kong. Retrospectively, Aerospace & Defence seem to be the industry with the least number of companies.

An interesting observation here is that the U.S. which dominated the list overall, does not seem to have an excessive influence on any one industry. At best there is indication of good performance in Diversified Financials. However, the U.S. does have a uniform and balanced presence across all the industrues unlike most countries. Click on each continent name above to view a highlighted version of the chart to better understand the market trend by industry.

Key Indicators

Moving forward we can see the 4 important indicators in million $ used by Forbes in determining the list. The breakdown of indicators by continent allows us to see some interesting features.


The Sales & Profits show a decent corelation between each other. From an economic point of view, when the profit margins are greater than the Sales, such as observed in Middle East and North America, this simply means the companies within the region are performing exceptionally well. Consequently, some regions such as Western Europe are infact the opposite with lesser profit margins than sales.


The Assets & Market Value indicators give us a surprising revalation; despite the U.S. having lesser than 30% of Global 2000 companies, the said companies make up more than 50% of the companies' Market Value. Market Value is a key indicator as it is the measure of market dominance. However, the East Asia tops the charts for having the most assets among all other regions.

Sales & Profits Overview

The heat map gives us a visual on what the general sales and profits balance is for Forbes 2000 companies. Noting that the values are in $ Millions , we can observe that the maximum count - 775 companies - have Sales of 0 to $10000 million and Profits of 0 to $5000 Million.

The clustering of smaller values around this large count is consistent with the general expectation whereby most companies would be within this range. However, there are some high-performing companies which have significantly larger profit and sales margins; visualised as outlying values.

Top 10 Forbes 2000 Companies

Contrary to expectation, the #1 company Berkshire Hathaway does not have the maximum Market Value in the top-10 list; Apple from the U.S. does. Upon observation we can see that only 4 countries make up the Top-10 Companies in the world. Out of this list, the U.S. unsurprisingly dominates with 5 companies hailing from the country.

It is also worth noting that the Banking industry makes up a large portion of these top-10 companies. The second most frequented industry in the top-10 list is Diversified Financials.

Retrospectively, the Forbes Global 2000 list is intriguing and multifaceted in a variety of aspects. Understanding key players in the global economy such as China and the U.S. which dominate the global market could lead us to better navigate economic, social, and political challenges.
